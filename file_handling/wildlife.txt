**India’s wildlife is among the richest in the world, with diverse habitats ranging from the Himalayas to coastal mangroves. Here’s a concise overview in bullet points (around 200 words):**
- 🌏 **Biodiversity Hotspots**: India is one of 17 *megadiverse countries*, hosting four global biodiversity hotspots—the Himalaya, Indo-Burma, Nicobar Islands, and Western Ghats.  
- 🌳 **Forest Cover**: About **24.6% of India’s land** is under forest, supporting ecosystems from tropical rainforests to alpine meadows.  
- 🐅 **Iconic Species**: India is home to the Bengal tiger, Asiatic lion, Indian elephant, one-horned rhinoceros, and snow leopard. These species symbolize conservation efforts and cultural heritage.  
- 🦜 **Bird Diversity**: Over **1,200 bird species** thrive here, including peacocks, hornbills, and migratory birds that visit wetlands like Bharatpur.  
- 🐍 **Reptiles & Amphibians**: India harbors **6.2% of global reptilian species** and **14.7% of amphibians**, many endemic to regions like the Western Ghats.  
- 🌊 **Aquatic Life**: Rivers and coasts support gharials, river dolphins, and diverse fish species, while mangroves like Sundarbans shelter unique fauna.  
- 🏞️ **Protected Areas**: India has **over 100 national parks and 500 wildlife sanctuaries**, including famous reserves like Jim Corbett, Kaziranga, and Gir.  
- ⚖️ **Conservation Challenges**: Habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict remain pressing issues, though initiatives like *Project Tiger* and eco-tourism help safeguard biodiversity.  
- 🌱 **Global Significance**: With **7–8% of all plant and animal species**, India plays a crucial role in global ecological balance.  

**In short, India’s wildlife reflects its vast ecological diversity, cultural reverence for nature, and ongoing struggle to balance conservation with development.**
