Lord Krishna is one of the most pivotal figures in Indian epics, especially the *Mahabharata*, where he embodies divine wisdom, diplomacy, and the eternal struggle for righteousness (dharma).  

Krishna in the Mahabharata
- **Divine Avatar**: Krishna is revered as the eighth incarnation of Lord Vishnu, born into the Vrishni clan. His early life includes legendary deeds such as slaying the tyrant king Kamsa and establishing the city of Dvaraka.  
- **Role in the Epic**: In the *Mahabharata*, Krishna is not a warrior on the battlefield but a **strategist, counselor, and charioteer**. He guides the Pandavas through the moral complexities of war and politics.  
- **Bhagavad Gita**: His most profound contribution is the dialogue with Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, known as the *Bhagavad Gita*. Here, Krishna teaches about duty, devotion, and the impermanence of life, emphasizing that one must act according to dharma without attachment to outcomes.  
- **Diplomat and Guide**: Krishna attempts peace negotiations with the Kauravas before the war, showing his commitment to avoiding bloodshed. Yet, when war becomes inevitable, he ensures the Pandavas uphold justice.
